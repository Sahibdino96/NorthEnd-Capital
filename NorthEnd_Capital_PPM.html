Acquisition Model

Why a Self-Funded Hybrid Buy-and-Build — and Why This Model Fits Us

01
We Are Not Searching Blindly
Our operating partner's consultancy has been serving dental practices in Greater Boston for 10 years. These 5 practices were identified through broker channels with full financial visibility before we committed a single dollar.
02
Buy-and-Build Creates Multiple Arbitrage
Individual practices trade at 5–7x EBITDA. A 5-practice regional platform trades at 9–11x. We capture that structural gap through simultaneous acquisition and operational improvement — not financial engineering.
03
Structure Aligns All Incentives
We acquire 75% of each practice. The retiring dentist retains 25% equity and a part-time salary — keeping them practicing, transitioning patients, and invested in the platform's success.

"NorthEnd Capital acquires majority stakes in 5 underperforming dental practices across Greater Boston, applies a proven operational playbook, and exits the consolidated platform to a DSO or PE buyer — generating superior risk-adjusted returns for investors."

07 — Part 3

Value Creation Plan

What We Do After We Buy — Three Phases Over 7 Years

Year 1 — Stabilize Months 1–12
  • Retain all clinical staff and maintain full patient continuity
  • Retiring dentist stays as 25% equity owner and part-time practitioner
  • Hire new full-time associate dentist on salary at each practice
  • Abdul's team takes over all non-clinical operations immediately
  • Implement KPI dashboard — revenue, patient volume, recall rates, collections
  • Protect cash flow — no major capital expenditure until operations stabilized
Years 2–3 — Optimize Months 13–36
  • Expand to 5 doctor days per week across all practices
  • Implement systematic annual fee schedule reviews
  • Drive digital marketing and online booking — grow new patient acquisition
  • Optimize hygiene recall — most stable recurring revenue in dentistry
  • Renegotiate lab and supply contracts at platform scale
  • Target EBITDA margin: 22–25% across portfolio
Years 4–7 — Scale & Exit Months 37–84
  • Centralize back-office across all 5 practices — one team, five locations
  • Build documented systems and processes — reduce key-person dependency
  • Achieve audited financials for clean exit process
  • Evaluate opportunistic bolt-on acquisitions if market conditions allow
  • Target exit to DSO or PE buyer at 9–11x consolidated EBITDA
  • Target exit value: $12.6M — $19.25M

The primary value creation lever — expanding doctor schedules from 3–4 days to 5 days — requires no new patients and no new equipment. The revenue uplift flows almost entirely to EBITDA given the fixed cost structure of dental practices. This is operational leverage at its most straightforward.
